a pathway to serendipity

By Minuki Medis

A pathway to serenity
is paved with broken glass,
It’s not smooth stones
or soft grass,
It’s the pain of memories, the sting of situational anxiety

It’s flooded with tears tasting of brine
of emotional cuts deeper than a ravine

It’s hot as volcanic rock
it’s cold as leather in the rain, tucked

A pathway to serenity
is not paved with serendipity
Unfair?
maybe
but we trudge this path
not to feel the bliss,
but to see our pain, heal our wounds
We cannot grow, cannot progress
without fingering the scars on our back
with love instead of distaste
and while it may not be paved in serendipity, it occurs mysteriously
If you close your eyes with your breath, it’ll surround you
in comforting wisps

So if you’re on this path
and you feel like giving up,
the weight of your fallen angel wings feels too much to bear,
I beg of you to take one more breath
and crawl your way to one more stone set
because after the first crawl, the next is easier,
and eventually you’ll be bounding,
in full grown steps

For the pathway to serenity, the stones of serendipity
is the pathway of life
and we cannot get there without taking every curve we need,
every detour and seemingly malicious mockery of humanity’s insanity,
every spot of uneven that makes us angry,
We need every step on this path
like rungs on a ladder
but far more vast
and each one shall result in a glimpse, of a magic that everlasts

I promise you
from the bottom of my heart,
nay, the bottom of my soul, for that is boundless,
that you won’t know it at first
but you’ll reach the elusive, invisible gates when you’re ready,
unknowingly, you’ll take a breath,
your lungs will fill with an angel’s tears,
your eyes will see the stardust swirling in the sky,
your feet will feel healed, your head will feel light,
you wont realize it, it’ll be hidden in plain sight, but once the air from the breath
has coursed through your veins,
penetrated into your deepest alcoves
and weaved through your stories and most broken nights
and finally come back onto your tongue to be re-released into the night sky,
with shooting stars and white twinkling lights
then,
then, my love,
you’ll know you have reached the true meaning of life
serendipity will surround you
in dazzling glory, in magnificent shine,

and serenity will finally sit by your heart and soul’s right side
