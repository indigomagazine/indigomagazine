Birds Still Sing and Flowers Still Bloom

written by saee j.
graphics by ethan t.
photography by keon o.
modeled by rachel a.

The first sense to return is touch. You feel cold, soft soil under your fingers and toes. It’s soothing, and you dig your fingers in further to ground yourself (literally, you think with an exhale too minute to be considered a chuckle).

You force your eyes open and immediately regret it after being assaulted by sharp, bright rays of sun. The sun never shines this brightly back home. In fact, it’s hardly ever even visible. How do these Earth creatures–Humans, you remind yourself–deal with it?

After a few more breaths, you sit up, checking yourself for any injuries. Your eyes shift to your vehicle, the sight of her crumpled up form increasing your exasperation. Great. You hadn’t meant to make Earth your destination–no one ever does–but it seems like you are stuck here for the foreseeable future.

Earth is a wasteland. The creatures–Humans!--are vile creatures. Violence and destruction runs through their veins. They were graced with the fortune of inhabiting the most nurturing planet in the galaxies and they ruined it. At least, that was what you had always been told. You had deliberately, and successfully, avoided Earth on all of your journeys. Until now. Fear climbs its way into your chest. You are alone, unarmed, and unprepared in the most dangerous place you know, amongst the most dangerous creatures you know.

You take a cursory glance around, taking mental inventory of your surroundings. You’re under a large, fluttering green structure with a long brown base. The sunlight flitters in through the gaps, warming your face. You instinctively turn toward it as if drawn in. The air seems lighter here than back home. It flows in and out of you much more easily. You marvel at the vibrant colors around you. Such a stark contrast to the metallic browns, grays, and blacks that you associate with home.

The sound of giggles catches your attention. Two small Humans chase each other nearby, their play unbothered by the appearance of a strange creature and her vehicle. You become acutely aware that you are very much in a public place, surrounded on all sides by Humans. The two little ones near you collapse on the ground, still giggling endlessly at their own game. A smile comes on your face unwarranted, until a moment later, a larger Human approaches the two little ones with a stern look on her face. Your smile drops. She grabs the little ones by their arms and pulls them up, speaking foreign words in a harsh tone. You knew it. The pleasantry of the little ones was an anomaly of some kind. Most Humans act like the grown one who seemed so angry and disturbed.

You scramble back to your vehicle, cowering behind it to stay hidden. A jarring sound behind you causes you to jump. Jerking around, you are greeted by a scene completely different from the gentle green haven you were just in. Hard concrete paves miles and miles of land and loud machines jet by at breakneck speed. Tall buildings stretch toward the sky, cold and industrial in appearance. Yes, this is the Earth you have heard about. The green haven behind you must be an anomaly just like the two little Humans chasing each other.

You decide the best way to navigate your way on this planet is to blend in as much as possible. The Humans don’t look very different from you. They have ten fingers and ten toes, like you. They have arms and legs and noses and eyes like you, too. You wonder what makes them so destructive as a species when their skin is as soft as yours, their hair as unruly.

You venture onto the smaller concrete path that seems to be for the walking Humans to keep them away from the vehicles speeding by. You hold your chin up and purposefully swing your arms, as if you are an everyday part of this busy city. As much as you try, though, you cannot hide the awe in your eyes as you take in a piece of this planet you have heard so many stories–no, warnings–about.

As you keep walking, you are surprised to see the same two little ones and the older Human you saw in the green haven, now a bit ahead of you. You automatically take a fearful step back, but you are shocked when you observe them. This time, there is no aggressive grabbing of arms or harsh voices. This time, there is the older Human cradling one of the little ones in her arms while gently grasping the hand of the other one. There are animated voices and gestures. There is another older male Human coming out of a nearby building, picking up the walking child, and…connecting his lips with the older female Human? You’re not sure what exactly you are witnessing, but it fills your chest with an unfamiliar feeling, simultaneously pleasant and aching.

The further you walk, the more you see multiple little Humans accompanied by one or two older Humans. You soon deduce that these are parents and children. Back home, these little ones would have been on their own years ago. You yourself have no memory of ever knowing a parent. Of course, that is probably the better practice, you surmise, considering you have only seen families together on Earth, and Earth is a model no one wants to follow. But that confusing feeling in your chest does not seem to agree.

Soon, the sun sets and darkness descends, and the city looks a lot more like home. But instead of being comforted by this familiarity, you suddenly find yourself missing the feeling of the sun illuminating your skin. If Earth was anything like home, it would be a long time before that happened again. But to your surprise, the city doesn’t remain dark for long. The concrete paths, towering buildings, and little shops become warmly lit, and although it doesn’t feel the same as the sun itself, you find yourself lingering in the block of light streaming out of a shop window.

As you peer into the shop, you find yourself locking eyes with a female Human inside who looks to be your age. She smiles and gestures to you and then to the entrance of the shop. You aren’t sure what she is trying to communicate, so you remain still. She comes to the entrance and says something to you. After more silence from you, she grabs your hand and pulls you in. She keeps chattering in the foreign language, unperturbed by your lack of response. The shop is warm compared to the brisk air outside, and the most pleasant aroma fills your nostrils. You automatically inhale deeply, savoring it. The Human sits you down on a fluffy structure even softer than the soil you found yourself waking up on hours ago. The shop is nearly empty, and you feel apprehensive about being trapped here with this stranger who finds no problem in leading you around. But the air outside is cold and the shop is bright and warm, so you stay. The Human bustles around behind a slab of marble, puttering with strange-looking machines. Soon, she is back in front of you with a container of hot, steaming liquid of some kind. She hands you the container with another beaming smile and another incomprehensible comment. You are wary of accepting anything from a stranger. But you are hungry and thirsty and the stranger’s smile is bringing back that bittersweet feeling in your chest, so you take a hesitant sip.

The longer you spend here, the more you discover. Earth is not what you were told about. Earth is cold and warmth. Earth is darkness and light. Earth is the sun and the moon. Earth is people, you learn. And the longer you are here, the more you understand about those people. People are families. People are affection, care, love. And yes, people can be destructive. People start wars and bulldoze trees and pave concrete over grass. People fight, but the birds still sing and flowers still bloom. And then people come together and amass resources for war victims and refugees. People plant more trees. People help the people help the people help the people. And perhaps that is what makes Earth more home than your own home ever was.

Years pass. You learn the languages of Earth. You travel beyond the piece of Earth you stumbled on and find more expansive green havens and more busy cities. You promise yourself that one day, you will return to your own planet, and all the others you have visited, and tell them about Earth. Tell them about its people and how it is the furthest thing from a wasteland. Tell them how they got it all wrong. You promise yourself that you will leave as soon as you find a reason to. But that reason never comes and one day, you take your last breath of the Earth’s air and are returned to the Earth’s soil, exactly where you were first born as Human.
