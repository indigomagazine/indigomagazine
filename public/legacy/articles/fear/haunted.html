Haunted

Written by Minuki Medis & Design by Victoria B.

Darling, they warn you as a child to be aware of dark forests, Don’t talk to the man on the corner of the street, Don’t stay out till too late
They tell you how to be safe, Tell you to stay armed with pepper spray and mace, That if you do all of this, stick by all of this Get down on your knees and worship the nuances of all of this
You, you will be safe
But what do you do when the darkness follows you home When it sits lurking in your head Wrapping itself around your gyri, calcifying in your heart Corrupting the veins, bending them into nooses for the brain When it makes you shiver, tremble, and shake Terrified of a night that may never break How do you breathe, how do you sit still? How do you ever find an ounce of peace? What is safe about this? Tell me what is safe about this? I beg of you, make something safe out of this
Because I cannot finish my food, nor can I sit alone in a room If I turn my head too briskly, the shadows seem humanoid I cannot trust my decisions, I cannot trust my senses Abilities turn to water, functionality turns to dust
What is worse, a haunted house or a haunted home?
Who are we without our mind: are we water, are we wine? Are we ethereal and sublime- even if my mind holds the decay and mold of an uncontrollable time?
These memories can be by enemies And thoughts are tridents for grievancy Preach to me how to make this pain go away - Creative destruction of the demons, but the demons won’t delay Re-equip, readjust, battleground of unconscious My pathetic prayers fall into pits of abyss
Yet despite the shaking and quivering in the dark The tear stains on kitchen tiles after midnight I hold my head high as I walk through the night Because fear may be real, fear may be scary The world outside may be dark and wary,
but nothing, absolutely nothing, can be worse than what’s inside The home I used to call my mind
