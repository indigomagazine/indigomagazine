an ode to my culture

By Sarah Rizvi

Golden strands fill the cracks of my skin.

You flow, glistening.
In the valleys of luster
nestled in the crevices of my body,
In the deep ridges of my ear,
In the rhythmic ups and downs of my chest,
The curves of my stomach.
You caress the top of my head with gentle hands,
Careful, as if not to startle me,
trailing down my spine,
leaving dark colored stars spilling in wake.

With a paintbrush carefully dipped
in a dark shimmering pigment,
you leave lines of color,
etching a map with no destination
into my skin.
The hollows of my eyes.

You leave a message
laced with the gold of my history.
Like a language made up,
between me and myself,
you whisper musical notes full of stories,
filling my ears with harmonies of a past I will never see.
The gentle smell of jasmine fills the open air around me,
woven into the strands of my plait,
Each piece of hair

another story to be read.
