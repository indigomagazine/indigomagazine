Hopes of familiarity

Story by Rebecca D. and Graphic Design by Silas S.

We run headfirst into the open arms of familiarity,
Running back home, reigniting old friendships,
Embracing the senses of memory.
Listening to music of times past, paging through yearbooks,
Getting sucked into a fuzzy nostalgia.


Then, there’s the shift.

Grasping onto old acquaintances,
We begin to drive ourselves mad
Itching and scratching for the way things were.
Trapped into some swirling pool of our own minds, and
Kept away from marching forward, alongside time.


The present slips away while we’re in this nostalgia,
Minutes tick away looking at old photographs
Refusing to embrace new challenges and new successes.
Eventually, the past alienates us from the present,
Unless we finally embrace life for today.
