Lovestruck

writing & photos by aisha a.

How must it feel?
What could it mean?
To be human?
Oh, to be free.

Void of wings and the commandments of the skies.
To devote one heart to another,
knowing that in a world so transient,
for such love there is always an end in sight.

Perhaps the angels are who they are,
because their questions are of such a simple nature.
Curious are they,
to understand how it sometimes seems,
that the softest of sin,
string together to create stories of love so holy.

Like the sweethearts of park benches and grassy fields,
cobblestone and dancing curtains,
undercover darkness,
moonlit to the touch.
Everything they do,
Just to feel real.

Their stories so raw, so earthly,
a glimpse of which could compromise even an angel from above.
So they say curiosity killed the cat.
Or perhaps it seems that the forbidden fruit was bitten,
just so a girl could laugh.
& it would be eaten to its core,
because there is something so alluring about love that is ill fated from the first glance,
that even a saint could crave.

This is where the pages turned.
This is when the clouds churned.
The valleys of innocence ran dry,
& from them an angel stepped into the sunlight.
Looking for love,
in all the places where such a thing she would never find.

It is quite difficult for a soul of pure nature, to differentiate between the whispers of lust,
or the deep eyes of devotion.
All the noise always sounds like music at first.
Until she has danced too hard.
Until there are too many ghosts in her heart's graveyard.

Father.
She will look to You then,
supplications of mercy and salvage rolling off her tongue.

The lips that kissed another, will now kiss the sky.
How she wishes to roam once again in Your gardens,
How she weeps again tonight

But she has become bound to the mud.
She has become,
Struck with love.
