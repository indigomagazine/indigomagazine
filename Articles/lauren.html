drowned.

Written By Lauren Lowndes

my brain
is filled with concepts.
projects.
stories.
people. friends. therapists.
idealized versions
of my bare bones reality- all of which
come to a standstill once my headphones come on.

my swarming brain begins to slow, lulling to a constant hum.
what was originally a murder of thoughts,
circling the remainder of my sanity cowering on the side of the road,
had become a tamed housepet.
crowing only on occasion, a lovable voice of companionship.

almost as if i'm floating, my mental rambling slow, slowing, a vast waterfall flowing to a meandering stream.
swirling clouds envelop my existence, a satisfying distraction from the insistent prodding of my brain.

eventually i must return to my tumultuous being, the turbulent thoughts suspended in zero
gravity in my head must rocket back down to earth- but in this moment, the violence is silent.
