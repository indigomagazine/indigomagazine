the seven

Story by Mia G. photo by Hitha G.

there is a painting in the town gallery
that tells the story
of the most horrific tragedies,
of the seven sins so deadly.

every detail is ensnaring,
captivating its observer
with faint whispers and the ghostly
feeling of a hand gripping your shoulder.


they say that to begin something in anger
is sure to end in shame
and that envy is a tie that
binds and binds until one cannot breathe.


they say that to be gluttonous is to
dig your own grave with your teeth
and that laziness is a disease that infects genuineness,
often replacing it with what is convenient.

they say that no one can be proud
when they are alone,
that the possession of pride will
make one the loneliest in a room full of people.


suddenly the whispers stop and the hand slips,
but your breath is shallow and your mind fuzzy,
a feeling of paranoia now deeply rooted in your bones.


there is a painting in the town gallery
that whispers the tragedies
of the seven sins so deadly and
denies the desired relief from its haunting story.
