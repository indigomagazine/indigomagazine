The little boy on the moon,
dangling his legs off the crescent,
looks down at us in wonder and
eagerly observes after a day of quiescence.

He sees two people at odds who
unexpectedly meet in the middle,
smiles overcoming their stern faces
as they realize they agree on just a little.

He watches as a mother and daughter slip out
from harsh fluorescents of a hospital and into the sun–
a butterfly flutters to the giggling girl’s cheek,
oblivious to her mother’s tears as she looks on.

He sees the moment friends become great,
connecting through heart and soul
and realize that such a connection
is so rare and can make someone whole.

He watches as mere strangers,
previously two ships passing in the night,
suddenly collide just before going over the edge
much to each other’s aching hearts’ delight.

When tomorrow starts without him,
he hopes the sun will shine brighter,
the world to turn a bit slower and
for the dim days to grow ever so lighter.

He hopes that the Fates will be
more generous than today,
removing splinters past and
allowing serendipity to slip in if it may.
