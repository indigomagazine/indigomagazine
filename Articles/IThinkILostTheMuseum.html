i think i lost the museum.

Story and design by shravya

I went to the art museum the other day
where i saw her,
she from my dreams. Upon her soft gaze lay
porcelain, acrylic knife sharps, soft pinkish lips
And I thought
how beautiful she is, how beautiful it is
to put pen to paper and draw
or maybe write it or
sing it or dance it
or act it - just express
the deep colors within me, deep oceans & wide expanses of
mind, tinted-glass bottling it all up and placing it on my shelf back
Home - that’s what you are to me,
pretty porcelain girl from the art museum.

When I returned to my desk, picked up my pencil,
waited under the rose petals with my
softest, prettiest smile for you,
porcelain girl,


the page was blank



the fog that filled my room was
foul, faint smell of burning forest fires.
the earth cries out for help but
it has no mouth, and
I have misplaced my ears.

I climbed into my car
but it had turned into mere lines.
my hands that clutched the steering
could not find anywhere to go.
my vision unravels,
this universe unravels.
Did i hurt you, porcelain girl? Why did you leave me
all alone
to suffer this … nothingness that
grips me so tightly, yet most frighteningly of all,
why do i feel nothing?

The dimensionless world holds no tearsholds no weather
holds no laughter
holds no colors holds no nature
holds no life holds no death holds no expression
holds no feelings holds nothing.


I don’t want to forget how to love you. My porcelain girl.
I don’t want to forget the colors & smells & sounds that make this world this world.
I don’t want to forget what it feels like to be human.

will you hold my hand one last time?


When the world comes to a close, will I have my art beside me?

When my love for art comes to a close, will the world still exist to me?


I’m sorry, porcelain girl goodbye.
