chained, we still blossom

Story by Amber D. Photos by hitha g.

I see those spangled banners flapping in the breeze. My existence doesn’t fit between those stars and lines. Our skin is demonized—we are called a disease. Having melanin is our only crime.

Free. What does that word mean to me? It’s an unkept promise, a broken record. There will never be a single, truthful guarantee that we will be free because our voices are ignored.

Chained and shackled, we still blossom. Our minds are abuzz with creation. Still, we want apologies and then some for the dehumanizing invasion.
